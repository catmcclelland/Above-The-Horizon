Draco

This constellation is visible all night long, every night of the year. It's name comes from the Latin translation which stands for dragon, so the constellation is known as 'Draco the Dragon'.

Andromeda

The Andromeda constellation is located in the northern sky. It was named after the mythical princess Andromeda. Other common names for Andromeda are the Chained Maiden, Persea, or Cephesis.

Orion

The Orion constellation is one of the brightest and most popular constellations in the night sky. Orion can be located on the celestial equator. Another common name for Orion is the Hunter.

Little Dipper

The Little Dipper contains Polaris, which is referred to as the northernStar or Pole Star. A larger constellation, known as Ursa Minor, surrounds the little dipper. Although the majority of Ursa Minor is made up of the little dipper.

DOrion's Belt

Orion's Belt is part of the larger constellation, Orion. Orion's Belt is made up of three bright stars, so it is easier to find Orion by first locating his belt.
